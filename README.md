Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common endocrine disorder affecting women of reproductive age worldwide. Early diagnosis remains a clinical challenge due to its heterogeneous symptoms and associated comorbidities. This study investigates uncertainty-aware deep learning approaches for PCOS prediction using real-world clinical datasets. We explore and compare three neural network-based models: a Monte Carlo Dropout Neural Network, a Bayesian Neural Network with Variational Inference (BNN), and a Deterministic Neural Network Ensemble. These models leverage comprehensive clinical, hormonal, ultrasound-derived, and lifestyle features, including biomarkers such as age, BMI, FSH, LH, AMH, PRL, TSH, β-HCG, ultrasound follicle size, and endometrial thickness, as well as factors like weight gain, hirsutism, skin darkening, and exercise habits. While all three models demonstrate strong predictive capabilities, the Bayesian neural network is particularly valuable due to its ability to provide principled uncertainty estimates, helping to identify cases where the model's confidence is lower. This characteristic is critical in medical diagnostics, where understanding model certainty can guide clinicians in making informed decisions and identifying cases requiring further evaluation. The findings highlight the potential of Bayesian deep learning methods to contribute to the development of trustworthy, uncertainty-aware decision support systems in gynecology and women’s healthcare.
